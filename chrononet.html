About ChronoNet

Built for the
curious.

ChronoNet is an internet time machine — a living archive of the web as it was, year by year, for anyone who wants to remember, discover, or just feel something.

"The internet has no memory. I gave it one."

The modern web is a palimpsest — layers upon layers of redesigns, 404 errors, and broken archives. Entire eras of culture have quietly vanished: the Flash games that ate your afternoons, the GeoCities pages someone poured their heart into, the memes that were everywhere and then nowhere.

ChronoNet reconstructs those lost moments not as musty museum pieces, but as living, breathing snapshots. I built this because the internet deserves a history that feels as alive as it once did. Because culture moves faster than memory, and I needed somewhere to slow down and look back.

Every year in our archive is curated by hand: real data, real cultural context, real artifacts. Not hallucinated, not approximated. The web as it genuinely was.

What I Stand For
Accuracy First
Every entry is researched and verified against primary sources. If I can't confirm it, I don't include it. The archive is only valuable if it's trustworthy.
Cultural Respect
Internet culture is real culture. I treat every era with the same rigor a historian would bring to any other period — because these moments shaped how billions of people think.
Open Access
History belongs to everyone. ChronoNet is free to explore, free to share, and free to use as a reference. No paywalls on the past.
